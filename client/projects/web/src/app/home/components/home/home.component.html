Our Origin Story

Origin Story

In 2015, Reena was on the board of the United Way (yes, she's kind of a big deal... or at least she was) and was helping to start a new initiative challenging young professionals to get involved in the community. That initiative turned into a massive "pitch" convention. (OK, it was more of a gala. Or like a small festival. Actually, it was a happy hour. And it wasn’t at a bar. And the drinks weren’t free.) The idea was to give a forum to anyone willing to pitch an idea to the community (also known as happy hour attendees), who would then vote on the ideas and the top 3 would be provided with United Way support to turn the idea into a reality.

In anticipation of this event, Reena and her colleague, PJ Hines, came up with the FoodWeb concept. Well, actually, PJ came up with the idea, but since Reena put him on the spot and demanded that he come up with something, she likes to take credit. PJ was struck by how much food was thrown out - at sporting events, meetings, networking opportunities - when it could have been given to people who were hungry. So, PJ pitched his idea: Let’s mobilize volunteer drivers to volunteer a few minutes of their time, on their own schedule, to take food from places that would throw it out and give it to people who will use it! The crowd went wild....and FoodWeb was born!

Well... not quite yet. While PJ (and Reena and lots of happy hour attendees) loved the idea, they needed a little help to put it into action. So... enter Shayan Ghose. Shayan and Reena go back (like...really back; Reena used to babysit Shayan, and according to her, she still has to babysit him). Reena knew that Shayan was back in the Buffalo area and looking for a project to sink his teeth into, and so she brought him into the “Web” (see how we did that?). Shayan was immediately intrigued... and dismayed that really good food was being wasted when he (and others, of course) could be eating it. And FoodWeb was born!

Actually... still not quite yet. Shayan put together a team of volunteer coders and software developers (i.e., really smart high school students who were willing to code for pizza) who spent a TON of time creating a website to coordinate donors, drivers, and receivers. Shayan then brought in Mark Nemmer (Shayan had to beg, it was pathetic), who became our fearless head of technology, to bring together all the hard work and get the website into final form! And surely then, FoodWeb was born!

Not exactly... Andrew Freedman, another colleague of Reena’s (see how she’s the lynchpin of all things??), heard about FoodWeb and immediately thought about how he could involve his clients (school districts) in the fun! So, he decided to join the team and focus on food recovery and volunteer opportunities with school districts. And then... FoodWeb was born!!!

Well... at least preliminarily. But the pandemic hit, and FoodWeb had to change its operations since, you know, there were no events to attend or food to collect and leaving the house in general seemed... unwise. So, the fearless team came up with the idea of weekly sandwich drives! The sandwich army (i.e., people stuck at home) would make sandwich lunches on a Saturday morning and drop them off to a local “hub” who would collect sandwiches and transport them to St. Luke’s Mission of Mercy for distribution to those who needed a lunch!

Shayan Ghose

 Executive Chairperson

Shayan grew up in Amherst and graduated from Williamsville North before completing his undergrad and masters education from Cornell University. He currently works in the financial sector. In his free time, he spends a bit too much time theorizing about Game of Thrones or watching the NBA and NFL. His friends know that he's always ready for a ping pong battle.

Shayan was first introduced to the idea of food rescue in WNY by PJ Hines and Reena Dutta at a Next Generation United event in 2017. The idea caught hold with him and a few months later FoodWeb was born with the mission of facilitating food donations with the use of technology.

Mark Nemmer

 Vice Chairperson and CTO

Mark works as a software developer at the University at Buffalo. He strives to improve and maintain various web apps across the university. In his free time he can be found hanging out with family and friends, playing video games, or programming.

Mark was introduced to the FoodWeb team by Shayan Ghose. He loves to code, and wishes to use his abilities to help others, so the position of technical lead is a great fit. Mark is excited to see FoodWeb thrive and bring about positive change within the community.
